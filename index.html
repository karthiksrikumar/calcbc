Overview
The Goal

Road to 270: Calculus BC Edition is a competitive board game for 2 to 4 players in which contestants race around a campaign-themed board, answering Calculus BC questions to earn movement and outmaneuver opponents through strategic squares, special events, and political chaos.

The sole objective: Be the first player to land exactly on the WINNER square. This game demands precision — overshoot it, and you keep going around the board for another lap. There are no shortcuts and no mercy. This game requires precision.

Precision matters. You must land exactly on the WINNER square to win. If your spin takes you past it, you continue moving and must complete another full lap of the board.
Components
What's in the Box
Game Board

One campaign trail board featuring all square types: Start, Winner, Jail, Person Card squares, Breaking News squares, Pac Perk squares, Primary Debate corners, and Convention corners.

Question Cards

45 Calculus BC multiple-choice question cards (A/B/C/D). Answers printed on the back. A full answer key is also included separately.

The Biden Wheel

One spinner numbered 1 through 6. Used to determine how many spaces a player moves forward after answering correctly.

Person Cards

A deck of Person Cards, each with a unique instruction. Identifiable by their matching cover card on every square.

Breaking News Cards

A deck kept at the center of the board. Drawn when landing on a Breaking News square after a correct answer.

Pac Perk Cards

A deck kept at the center of the board. Drawn when landing on a Pac Perk square after a correct answer.

Player Pieces

Game pieces in distinct colors. Note: the color of your piece may carry certain implications... (wink).

Debate Slips

Two matching pairs of paper slips, one pair stored in each corner's pocket (Primary Debate and Convention corners).

Before You Play
Setup
  1. All players agree on one person to serve as the Timer. The Timer's sole responsibility during the game is to time 75 seconds for each player's turn. The Timer does not have any other special authority — they still play the game normally.
  2. The Timer picks their game piece first, choosing from the available colors. Remember: the color of your piece could carry certain implications (wink).
  3. Piece selection then continues clockwise from the Timer, with each player picking one piece in turn order until all players have a piece.
  4. Place all pieces on the START square. This is where every player begins.
  5. Shuffle the Question Card deck and place it face-down in its designated area. Shuffle the Person Card deck, Breaking News deck, and Pac Perk deck separately and place each at the center of the board.
  6. Confirm the Biden Wheel is accessible to all players. The Biden Wheel is numbered 1 to 6.
  7. The player order established during piece selection (Timer, then clockwise) is the turn order for the entire game. The Timer goes first.
Turn Order Reminder: The Timer always goes first. All other players follow in clockwise order from the Timer's left for the entire game.

Gameplay
How a Turn Works

Each turn follows the same sequence. Pay close attention — the rules for what happens after you answer depend entirely on whether you are correct or incorrect.

Step 1 — Draw a Question Card

Pick up the top card from the Question Card deck. The moment you pick up the card, the Timer starts the 75-second clock. You may not put the card down, pass, or delay — the clock is running.

The instant the card is lifted, the timer begins. There is no grace period. You have 75 seconds from that moment to submit your answer.

Step 2 — Answer the Question

Within 75 seconds, you must declare your answer aloud: A, B, C, or D. Only one answer will be accepted. Your answer is locked once spoken.

After answering (or when time expires), the card is flipped to reveal the answer on the back. The full answer key is also available for verification. The used card is then placed at the bottom of the Question Card deck.

Step 3a — If You Got It RIGHT

  1. Spin the Biden Wheel. Wherever Biden's nose lands is your number — that is how many spaces forward you move.
  2. Move your piece forward the indicated number of spaces.
  3. When you land on a square, pick up that square's card (Person Card, Breaking News, or Pac Perk) and follow its instructions.

Step 3b — If You Got It WRONG

  1. Spin the Biden Wheel. Wherever Biden's nose lands is your number — you move that many spaces backward.
  2. Move your piece backward the indicated number of spaces.
If you answer incorrectly: you move backward, and you do NOT pick up or interact with any card on the square you land on — regardless of its type.
Movement Rules
How Movement Works

Movement At a Glance

Correct
Spin Biden Wheel → move that many squares FORWARD → flip the card on your landing square
Incorrect
Spin Biden Wheel → move that many squares BACKWARD → do NOT interact with any card
Start Line
You cannot move backward past the Start square. If backward movement would take you there or beyond, your piece stops at Start and waits.
Jail
Your piece is placed on Jail. You are skipped next turn. Special exit rules apply (see Jail section).

The Start Line Boundary

Once you have passed the Start square for the first time, you can never move backward past it again. If a backward move would land you on Start or would carry you before it, your piece stops on the Start square and stays there. You do not pick up any card. You simply wait for your next turn.

Passing the Winner Square

If your forward movement carries you past the Winner square, you do not stop there — you continue counting squares forward and keep going around the board. You must complete another full lap and attempt to land exactly on Winner on a future turn. There is no option to "bank" extra movement.

Cards and Used Cards

After any card (Question Card, or a math/other card drawn from a square) is used, it is placed at the bottom of its respective stack. Decks are never reshuffled mid-game; used cards recycle from the bottom.

Board Squares
Types of Squares

The board contains the following square types. All card interactions described below only apply when you have answered a question correctly and moved forward onto that square.

Person Card Squares

Each Person Card square has a cover card on top — it displays the same art as the Person Card beneath it, so you always know it is a Person Card square. When you land on one (after a correct answer), pick up the cover card to reveal the Person Card beneath and follow its instructions.

Example Person Card
Critical Rule — Secondary Movement from Person Cards: If a Person Card instructs you to move forward or backward to another square, you do NOT flip or interact with the card on the square you are sent to. The card on your new landing square is left untouched.

Person Cards may instruct you to do various things: move forward, move backward, skip a turn, go to Jail, or other special actions. Read the card carefully and follow its exact instructions. After use, place the card at the bottom of the Person Card stack.

Breaking News Card Squares

When you land on a Breaking News square after a correct answer, take the top card from the Breaking News deck at the center of the board. Read it aloud and follow its instructions. After use, place it at the bottom of the Breaking News deck.

Pac Perk Card Squares

When you land on a Pac Perk square after a correct answer, take the top card from the Pac Perk deck at the center of the board. Read it aloud and follow its instructions. After use, place it at the bottom of the Pac Perk deck.

One Rule for All Card Squares: Breaking News and Pac Perk cards are only drawn when you answered correctly and moved forward onto that square. Landing there after an incorrect backward move means you draw nothing.

Start Square

The square where all players begin. It also serves as the backward-movement boundary — no player may move behind it. If you are sitting on Start waiting, you begin your turn normally (draw a question card).

Winner Square

The winning square. You must land here exactly to win the game. If your spin overshoots it, keep moving forward — you complete another lap.

Special Corners
The Primary Debate & The Convention

There are two special corner squares on the board. Each functions identically, though they are named differently. These corners host a Thumb War event that can dramatically shift positions on the board.

Corner 1: Primary Debate

Located at the first corner of the board. Contains a pocket holding two matching slips of paper.

Corner 2: The Convention

Located at the second corner of the board. Contains a pocket holding two matching slips of paper. Same rules apply.

How the Thumb War Works

  1. The first two players to land on or pass the corner square each take one slip of paper from the corner's pocket.
  2. Those two players then conduct a thumb war — wherever their pieces currently are on the board does not matter. The event is resolved immediately upon the second player taking their slip.
  3. The winner of the thumb war moves their piece 1 square forward from their current position.
  4. The loser of the thumb war moves their piece 2 squares backward from their current position.
The thumb war movement is NOT a regular turn move. You do not spin the Biden Wheel. You do not flip any cards on the squares you move to as a result of thumb war movement.
Third and fourth players: Once both slips have been claimed, the corner event is over. Players who arrive after both slips are gone simply pass through the corner with no effect.

Special Square
Jail

Jail is a special square that halts a player's progress. A player is sent to Jail by the instruction on a Person Card (or any other card that explicitly says so).

When You Are Sent to Jail

  1. Move your piece to the Jail square immediately.
  2. You are skipped entirely for the next round of turns (one full pass around the table). You do not draw a question card during the skipped turn.

Your Next Turn — Attempting to Leave

  1. At the start of your next eligible turn (after the skip), you spin the Biden Wheel before drawing any question card.
  2. If you spin a 1: you are released to Just Visiting — the designated area on the same Jail square — and your turn ends there. You draw no question card this turn.
  3. If you spin any number other than 1: you are sent to the square called Island Time. You do not flip or interact with any card on Island Time. Your turn ends.
Just Visiting vs. Jail: Just Visiting is a safe position on the same square as Jail. A player in Just Visiting is not in Jail — they take their next turn normally, with no spin-to-escape requirement.
When sent to Island Time from Jail, you do NOT pick up or interact with any card on Island Time, regardless of what type of square it is.

Victory
Winning the Game

The first player to land exactly on the WINNER square wins the game immediately. The game ends at that moment — no other players complete the round.

Exact landing only. If your Biden Wheel spin takes you past the Winner square, your piece continues moving forward and you must complete another full lap. There is no option to stop short. Precision is everything.

Congratulations — you've made it to 270. The campaign is over. The road was long, the calculus was hard, and you earned it.
